Hey there !

Growing up in Indonesia, I had always aspired to become a software engineer. I started learning Visual Basic when I was in middle school, developing custom text editors and web browsers. Later on, I jumped into developing student registration systems for my high school. I eventually moved to the United States in 2017, attended Austin Community College for a while, and transferred to UW-Madison where I will be graduating in May 2021.

Currently, I am working on AI/ML research for the NASA Earth-Venture Instrument PREFIRE mission; developing models to accurately predict cloud cover. I am also working on a full-stack application to automate NMR data processing. Since giving back is an important part of what I do, I am currently involved in Google's Developer Student Clubs at UW-Madison where I design marketing materials and host web development workshop tutorials.

My current goal is to jump into the industry to gain more experience that would help hone my skills in software engineering and communication. With these skills, I would like to eventually found and lead a non-profit organization that focuses on helping children from developing nations to jump into technology. That is why I am very humbled to announce that I will be joining Bloomberg LP as a software engineer post-graduation. Bloomberg LP has a long history of giving back to the community as it encourages its employees to do volunteer work and donates most of their revenue to philanthropy. Their culture fits with my personal goals and that is why I am excited to be starting this journey with them.

Portfolio

PREFIRE Website



I was initially hired to design a website for an upcoming NASA JPL instrument mission to showcase the mission objectives and any new upcoming updates. The design is fairly simple, it is a one page site where you can consume all of the information easily just by scrolling down. I chose to do it this way to make sure that the information in the website is easily readable seeing that the target audience is non-experts. Another interesting feature of the site is the parallax effect of the background. I designed it this way to achieve the effect of depth, just like how if a satellite were to orbit the earth and look down, everything closer to it will seem to move faster than the earth ground itself. The backgrounds used were chosen carefully to imitate a satellite's view of the earth. Visit the full site here.

Garuda Bisa Website



I was asked to redesign Garuda Bisa's website with the main goal of making it more readable and clean. One of the important things that I did initially was to remove any and all information on the old website that is redundant. With only a few crucial information left, I started thinking on how to represent the information visually. For example, the how to apply part, I decided that it would be better to make it only three steps and complement those steps with a relevant background image.
